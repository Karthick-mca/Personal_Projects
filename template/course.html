List of Provided Courses!

Here are some common courses students take in law school or college to obtain a law degree:

    -> Constitutional Law: Study of the U.S. Constitution, its interpretation, and its application to various legal issues.

    -> Contracts: Principles and rules governing the formation, performance, and enforcement of contracts.

    -> Torts: Laws related to civil wrongs and injuries, including negligence, intentional torts, and strict liability.

    -> Property Law: Rights and obligations related to real and personal property, including ownership, possession, and transfer.

    -> Civil Procedure: Rules and processes for conducting civil lawsuits in court.

    -> Criminal Law: Definition of crimes, criminal liability, and defenses.

    -> Criminal Procedure: Rules and processes for conducting criminal trials and investigations.

    -> Evidence: Rules and principles governing the admissibility of evidence in court proceedings.

    -> Legal Writing and Research: Developing skills in legal analysis, writing, and research.

    -> Professional Responsibility: Study of ethical standards and rules governing the legal profession.
